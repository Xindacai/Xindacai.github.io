Reponse 2

During my two minutes with the experience, my discrete actions were as follows:

Action 1:

I saw the website's front page with the artist’s statement. I played around with the cursor and saw the color of its text and background and the keywords in the paragraph changed depending on where I moved the cursor on my screen.

Action 2:

Scroll down to see another page with a purple background and a white triangle pointing upwards in the middle, and inside the triangle, write three words, “div center Perfectly” from top to bottom.

But when I moved my cursor below the triangle, the shape and the words inside it performed a smooth animation where the triangle spun to point downwards, and the order of the words from top to bottom also changed to “Perfectly div center”. When I move my cursor above again, the triangle and the words inside it will return to their original position and I spent some time playing with this animation.

Action 3:

I scrolled further down to the third page with a black background. This page shows a pixel animation design with the title “Pixel Displacement Effect” written in 3D text.

I spent some time playing with this animation where the small pixel blocks that built this picture would move away from wherever I moved my cursor. So if I move my cursor to the middle of the picture, it will leave a hole there when the pixel blocks scatter away from my cursor.

Action 4:

I scrolled down to the final page of the website where it showed another few lines of text, and I played around with their animations. Each line of text performs a unique animation, where the text colors and the positions of each letter change differently when I put my cursor on each of them.

Action 5:

At the very bottom of the webpage, there are a few lines of text saying “email”, “line” and “LinkedIn” that are clickable links. So I clicked on those links to find the artist’s email address, their QR code for adding friends in the LINE app, and their LinkedIn account page.

Reponse 3

The part of the experience I spent the most time engaging with was the pixel displacement effect. Its title text has a unique layout as every letter in those words is placed in a different angle. This effect presents an image of a painting or perhaps some sort of cartoon work which is built up with a large amount of small pixel blocks. I was fascinated by the high levels of smoothness and fluidity of the pixel animation in this effect. When I move through the picture with my cursor, the pixel blocks that constructed it move away from my cursor quickly and smoothly with no lag.

The speed and distance that the pixel blocks which move away from my cursor also depends on how quickly I move the cursor through the picture. If I move it through slower, the block seems to move away slower but further from my cursor. If I move it through quicker, the block seems to move away faster but less distance away from my cursor. So I spent a lot of time trying to move through the picture with my cursor at different speeds to see how good the animation would look during each attempt.

Reponse 4

During my two-minute interaction with the experience, my most common action was moving the cursor around or on top of texts or objects that existed throughout the webpage, to repeatedly play around with all of their animations. Through this, I wanted to closely examine how they work and in what exact ways the objects and texts will move around during the animation. Through my repeated attempts at playing with the animations, I wanted to find out whether the direction in which I move my cursor on the screen could make each animation appear differently both in terms of color and movement change.

I also performed this action most commonly through my interaction with the experience because they are the most enjoyable for me and I feel like they are the main attractions for this entire experience.

Reponse 5

In my impression, the intended primary goal of the interactive experience is just to entertain its users and amaze them with a fancy webpage design filled with bright colors and advanced text and object animations everywhere. The page is very short with only three or four pages and it doesn’t provide useful information about any topics, nor does it have a search function. So it’s clear that this interactive experience is not for any formal uses like learning, education, or research. Therefore, the main purpose of this interactive experience is just a piece of art that intends to make users have a fun time and be attracted by its visual design when using it.

Reponse 6

The interactive experience communicates its primary goal through its design style and structure. Unlike most webpages that are used for educational, shopping, or job-finding purposes which usually have a neat and simple layout with a white background to appear formal. The design of this webpage is very abstract and unpredictable as every page inside it has a completely different style of design and all of their background colors and fonts are different.

In addition, every group of texts and object has their unique animation. This type of design would look very messy on a webpage that has a formal usage, but highly engaging when simply trying to create a piece of art with no intent for practical uses. Through this, the artist of this experience communicates their idea that this webpage is only meant for fun and they only want users to focus on the creative aspects of it rather than any information or content it provides.

Reponse 7

My impression is that the design encourages ongoing interactions by encouraging its users to try out all of the animations on the texts, shapes, and objects that are present throughout the webpage and experiment with how they could make each animation appear slightly different in terms of color change or movement depending on the different actions (e.g. moving their cursors in different directions) they perform on the assets.

The interactive experience is mainly designed for short-term or one-time engagement rather than long-term because its content is too short with only around three pages and doesn’t provide any useful information. So the users might appreciate the creative design of this interactive experience when they first access it, but this experience wouldn’t be useful in the long term as it is not designed to assist users in their daily lives or work, but purely for one-time entertainment.

Reponse 8

The website’s mechanisms and features are attractive to the users, but they generally don’t encourage users to return or engage with them repeatedly. While its animations are fun to play with, the website isn’t designed to help its users in any aspect of their daily lives or make them improve at any skill. So there isn’t any need for users to repeatedly engage with it regularly.

In addition, unlike websites that would save users’ progress or track their past activities and provide them with new content based on these records every time the users log back on, the content on this website always stays the same. So while the users might find its features interesting for the first time, they will eventually get bored.
